How to Generate Your Google Review Link

Before you can send review requests, you need your unique Google review link.

Step 1: Find Your Google Business Profile

Go to Google and search for your business name + city (e.g., "ABC Flooring Atlanta").

Your Google Business Profile should appear on the right side of the search results.

Step 2: Get Your Place ID

Click "Share" on your Google Business Profile.

Copy the URL (it will look something like this: https://maps.app.goo.gl/abc123).

Go to this website: https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/places/web-service/place-id

Paste your Google Maps URL and retrieve your Place ID.

Step 3: Generate Your Review Link

Use this format:

https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID_HERE

Replace YOUR_PLACE_ID_HERE with your actual Place ID.

Example:

https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=ChIJN1t_tDeuEmsRUsoyG83frY4
Step 4: Shorten the Link (Optional)

Use a URL shortener like Bitly or TinyURL to make the link cleaner and easier to track.

Example shortened link:

https://bit.ly/ABCFlooringReview
TEMPLATE 1: STANDARD REVIEW REQUEST (Sent Immediately After Post-Install Call)
When to use:
Right after you hang up from the post-installation call, while the customer is still thinking about the conversation.
"Hi [Customer Name], thanks again for choosing us! Here's the link to leave your review: [Google Review Link]. It'll only take a minute, and it really helps us out. We appreciate you!"
Why this works:
  • Short, simple, and direct.
  • The link is right there—no friction.
  • Friendly tone without being pushy.
TEMPLATE 2: "PAY IT FORWARD" VERSION (Emphasizes Helping Others)
When to use:
Same as Template 1, but this version frames the review as altruistic (helping other customers), not transactional.
"Hi [Customer Name], thanks again for trusting us with your project! If you have a minute, we'd love it if you could leave us a quick Google review. When you do, you're helping the next family who's trying to decide where to go—kind of like paying it forward. Here's the link: [Google Review Link]. We really appreciate you!"
Why this works:
  • Reframes the request from "help me" to "help others."
  • Customers are more willing to do something altruistic than transactional.
  • Still includes the link for easy action.
TEMPLATE 3: FOLLOW-UP REMINDER (3–5 Days Later)
When to use:
If the customer hasn't left a review within 3–5 days of your initial text.
"Hi [Customer Name], just checking in—did the link I sent work okay? I know things get busy, but if you get a chance, we'd love that review! Here's the link again: [Google Review Link]. Let me know if you have any trouble with it. Thanks!"
Why this works:
  • You're not saying, "Hey, you forgot." You're saying, "Did the link work?"
  • It's a helpful, low-pressure nudge.
  • The actionable question ("did the link work?") prompts a response and reduces customer guilt.
TEMPLATE 4: AFTER YOU'VE FIXED A PROBLEM
When to use:
After you've resolved a small issue the customer reported (e.g., a crooked transition strip, a spot that needed touch-up, etc.).
"Hi [Customer Name], just wanted to let you know we took care of that [issue]. Everything should be perfect now! If you're happy with how we handled it, we'd love it if you could leave us a Google review. Here's the link: [Google Review Link]. Thanks for giving us the chance to make it right!"
Why this works:
  • Customers often leave glowing reviews after you fix a problem—because it shows you care.
  • You're framing the review as proof that you stand behind your work.
TEMPLATE 5: FOR CUSTOMERS WHO AREN'T TECH-SAVVY
When to use:
If the customer mentioned during your call that they're not comfortable with technology or don't know how to leave a review.
"Hi [Customer Name], here's the link to leave your Google review: [Google Review Link]. It's really easy—just click the link, tap the stars to rate us, and write a sentence or two about your experience. If you have any trouble, just call me and I'll walk you through it. Thanks!"
Why this works:
  • You're acknowledging their concern and making it feel easy.
  • You're offering to help if they get stuck.
TEMPLATE 6: FOR CUSTOMERS WHO SAID "I'LL DO IT LATER"
When to use:
If the customer said "I'll do it later" during your post-installation call.
"Hi [Customer Name], here's the link to leave your review whenever you're ready: [Google Review Link]. It only takes about 60 seconds. Thanks so much!"
Why this works:
  • You're honoring their request to do it later, but you're putting the link in their hands immediately.
  • You're emphasizing how quick it is (60 seconds).
TEMPLATE 7: FOR HIGH-VALUE OR VIP CUSTOMERS
When to use:
For customers who spent a lot of money, referred other people, or had an exceptional experience.
"Hi [Customer Name], I just wanted to say thank you again for trusting us with your project. It was a pleasure working with you! If you have a minute, we'd be honored if you'd leave us a Google review. Here's the link: [Google Review Link]. Thanks so much!"
Why this works:
  • The tone is more personal and appreciative.
  • "We'd be honored" elevates the request and makes it feel special.
TEMPLATE 8: FOR CUSTOMERS WHO REFERRED SOMEONE
When to use:
If the customer has already referred a friend, family member, or neighbor to you.
"Hi [Customer Name], thanks so much for referring [Referral Name] to us—we really appreciate it! If you have a minute, we'd love it if you could also leave us a Google review. It's kind of like referring us to a hundred people at once! Here's the link: [Google Review Link]. Thanks again!"
Why this works:
  • You're acknowledging their referral first (which makes them feel good).
  • You're reframing a Google review as a "mass referral."

Pro Tips for Text Message Success

1. Send the Text Immediately

Don't wait. Don't say "I'll send it later." Send it while you're on the phone with the customer or within 60 seconds of hanging up.

2. Use Their First Name

Personalization matters. Always include the customer's first name in the text.

3. Keep It Short

Text messages should be short and scannable. Don't write a paragraph—get to the point.

4. Include the Link in Every Text

Don't make them search for it. Put the link right there so they can click it immediately.

5. Follow Up Once (and Only Once)

If they don't leave a review within 3–5 days, send one follow-up text. After that, let it go. You don't want to annoy them.

6. Use a Business Texting Platform (If Possible)

If you're texting from a personal phone, use a business texting app like:

  • Podium
  • SimpleTexting
  • TextMagic
  • Twilio

These platforms let you:

  • Track who opened the text
  • See who clicked the link
  • Schedule follow-ups automatically
7. Track Your Response Rate

Keep a simple spreadsheet or dashboard that tracks:

  • How many review request texts you sent
  • How many customers left a review
  • Your response rate (goal: 40–60%)
Goal: 40–60% response rate

If your response rate is lower than 40%, troubleshoot:

  • Are you sending the text immediately?
  • Are you following up?
  • Is your link working?
  • Are you making the post-installation call first (to catch issues before asking for a review)?

What NOT to Do

❌ Don't Send a Text Without Calling First

The post-installation call is critical. It prevents "Silent Death" and sets you up for a positive review. If you skip the call and just send a text, your response rate will be much lower.

❌ Don't Send Multiple Follow-Ups

One follow-up is enough. After that, let it go. You don't want to annoy the customer or damage the relationship.

❌ Don't Use All Caps or Too Many Exclamation Points

It looks desperate and spammy. Keep your tone professional and friendly.

❌ Don't Ask Them to Leave a Review on Multiple Platforms

Focus on Google first. Once you have 100+ Google reviews, then you can branch out to Facebook, Yelp, etc. But don't overwhelm customers by asking them to leave reviews on five different sites.

❌ Don't Forget to Thank Them

When a customer leaves a review, send them a thank-you text or call them. It reinforces the behavior and strengthens the relationship.

Example thank-you text:

"Hi [Customer Name], I just saw your review—thank you so much! We really appreciate you taking the time to share your experience. If you ever need anything, don't hesitate to reach out. Thanks again!"

Sample Review Request Workflow (Start to Finish)

Here's what the full process looks like:

Day 0: Plant the Seed (During the Sale)

"By the way, if we do a great job for you, I'll ask you to leave us a quick review on Google. Fair enough?"

Day 1–2: Installation

The job gets done. Your installer does great work and cleans up thoroughly.

Day 2–3: Post-Installation Call

You call the customer 24–48 hours after installation:

"Hi [Customer Name], just checking in—how's the floor looking? Any concerns I can help with?" [Customer says everything's great] "That's great to hear! I'm going to text you a review link right now. Does that work?"
Day 2–3: Send the Text (Immediately)

You hang up and immediately send:

"Hi [Customer Name], thanks again for choosing us! Here's the link to leave your review: [Google Review Link]. It'll only take a minute, and it really helps us out. We appreciate you!"
Day 5–7: Follow-Up Text (If They Haven't Left a Review)

If they haven't left a review by Day 5–7, send one follow-up:

"Hi [Customer Name], just checking in—did the link I sent work okay? I know things get busy, but if you get a chance, we'd love that review! Here's the link again: [Google Review Link]. Thanks!"
Day 7+: Let It Go

If they still haven't left a review after the follow-up, let it go. Don't send a third text.

When They Leave the Review: Thank Them

As soon as you see the review come in, send a thank-you text or call them:

"Hi [Customer Name], I just saw your review—thank you so much! We really appreciate you taking the time. If you ever need anything, don't hesitate to reach out!"

Conclusion

Text messages are the fastest, easiest way to get Google reviews—if you do it right.

Your job is to:

  • Make the post-installation call first (to prevent "Silent Death").
  • Send the text immediately (with the link).
  • Follow up once (3–5 days later).
  • Thank the customer when they leave a review.
If you do that consistently, you'll capture reviews from 40–60% of your customers.

And over 90 days, that adds up to 50–100+ new Google reviews.
