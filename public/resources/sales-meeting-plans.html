SALES MEETING PLANS FOR REVIEW LEVERAGE

Ready-to-Use Agendas for Weekly Sales Meetings (Weeks 1–12 + Ongoing)

Introduction: Why Consistent Sales Meetings Matter

Reviews don't capture themselves. Your team needs consistent reinforcement, celebration, coaching, and accountability.

Most managers know they SHOULD talk about reviews in team meetings—but they don't know:

  • What to say
  • How long to spend on it
  • How to make it engaging (not just a lecture)
  • How to balance celebration with accountability

This document solves that problem.

It gives you 12 ready-to-use sales meeting agendas (one for each week of the first 90 days) that integrate review capture into your weekly sales rhythm—plus a maintenance mode agenda for Weeks 13 and beyond.

Each agenda includes:
  • Time allocation (5–60 minutes depending on the week)
  • What to say (scripts and talking points)
  • What to track (leaderboard, metrics, progress)
  • What to celebrate (wins, milestones, top performers)
  • What to practice (role-plays, objection handling)
  • What to troubleshoot (bottlenecks, resisters, drift)
Page 1

How to Use This Document

  • Weeks 1–4: Longer meetings (15–60 minutes) to train, practice, and build the habit.
  • Weeks 5–12: Shorter check-ins (5–15 minutes) to celebrate wins, troubleshoot bottlenecks, and maintain momentum.
  • Weeks 13+: Maintenance mode (5–10 minutes/week) to keep the habit alive without over-managing.

You can run these as standalone "review meetings" OR (better yet) integrate them into your existing weekly sales meetings (recommended—it shows reviews are part of the sales process, not a separate initiative).

The 12-Week Meeting Plan

WEEK 1: KICKOFF MEETING (60 minutes—one-time investment)
Goal: Explain the "why," introduce the 4-step process, announce incentives, address objections, and get verbal buy-in from the entire team.
When to hold it: Monday or Tuesday of Week 1 (first thing in the morning if possible)
Who attends: Sales team, installers (if possible), office staff (anyone involved in the customer experience)

AGENDA (60 minutes):

1. Explain the "Why" (15 minutes)

What to say:

"Alright team, we're launching a new system to capture more Google reviews. Here's why this matters—and why it's worth your time. Right now, we're spending [$X per month] on marketing. Most of those leads are cold—they've never heard of us, they're shopping price, and they're skeptical. That makes your job harder. Meanwhile, our competitors with 200+ reviews are getting leads for free—and those leads already trust them before they even call. Here's the data—and this is real, proven data from a high-performing flooring dealer who implemented this exact system: • Digital leads tripled when they started capturing reviews consistently. • 70% of their phone calls came from their Google Business Profile—not paid ads. • Total lead volume increased 30–40% during review-active months (typically building toward this level over 6-12 months as the review count reaches critical mass). This isn't theory. This is what happens when you build a review-first marketing engine. And here's the thing: this isn't just about marketing. It's about culture, morale, and attracting top talent. When we have 300+ five-star reviews, we become the company everyone wants to work for—and the company every customer wants to hire. But here's the hidden benefit most people don't talk about: This system saves us time and mental energy. When we have a strong review presence: • We're not chasing down cold leads or babysitting skeptical customers through 8 follow-ups. • We're not constantly deciding which marketing channel to bet on next (Facebook? Google? Mailers? Billboards?). • We're not burning 192–252 hours per year managing 20+ marketing channels. That's 18–32 work days per year we get back. We can use that time to train the team, improve operations, or just breathe. And for those of you on commission: more reviews = more warm leads = easier sales = higher commissions. Every review you capture makes your job easier and your paycheck bigger. So this isn't just good for the company. It's good for you."

2. Introduce the 4-Step Process (20 minutes)

What to say:

"Okay, so how do we actually capture reviews? It's simple. There are four steps—and the whole thing takes less than 3 minutes per customer. Here's the process: Step 1: Plant the seed (during the sale) During or at the end of the sales consultation, say: 'By the way, one of the ways we've grown is through word of mouth and online reviews. If we do a great job for you—and I'm confident we will—I'll ask you to leave us a quick review on Google. Fair enough?' Time investment: 10 seconds. Step 2: Post-installation call (24–48 hours after the job) Call the customer 24–48 hours after installation to check in and make sure everything went smoothly. Why? Because not all unhappy customers complain. Some will smile, say everything's fine, and then quietly decide never to refer you—or worse, leave a bad review months later. We call this 'Silent Death.' The post-installation call prevents Silent Death. You're catching small issues early and fixing them before they turn into big problems. Here's the script: 'Hi [Customer Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. I'm just calling to check in and make sure everything went smoothly with your installation. How's the floor looking? Any questions or concerns I can help with?' If everything's great: 'That's great to hear! I'm so glad we could take care of you. Now, I know you're probably busy, but if you have a minute this week, I'd really appreciate it if you could leave us a quick review on Google. It helps us out a ton and helps other people feel confident choosing us. I'm going to text you a link right now so it's easy—does that work?' If there's a small issue: 'Thanks so much for letting me know. I'm going to get that taken care of right away. Let me call you back once it's fixed, and then I'll send you that review link. Sound good?' Time investment: 2 minutes. Step 3: Text the link immediately (while you're still on the phone) Don't wait. Don't say 'I'll send it later.' Send the text while you're talking to them. Text template: 'Hi [Customer Name], thanks again for choosing us! Here's the link to leave your review: [Google Review Link]. It'll only take a minute, and it really helps us out. We appreciate you!' Time investment: 30 seconds. Step 4: Follow up (3–5 days later if they haven't left a review) Life gets busy. People forget. A friendly reminder is not pushy—it's helpful. Follow-up text: 'Hi [Customer Name], just checking in—did the link I sent work okay? I know things get busy, but if you get a chance, we'd love that review! Here's the link again: [link]. Thanks!' Time investment: 30 seconds. Total time per customer: 3 minutes, 10 seconds. That's less time than it takes to follow up on a cold lead three times. And every review you capture brings us more warm leads next month. Now, I'm going to hand out the scripts. You'll have them at your desk so you can reference them anytime you need." [Hand out laminated scripts or binders with the Review Capture Scripts file.]

3. Launch the Incentive Program (10 minutes)

What to say:

"Okay, so here's what's in it for you—directly. Starting today, you'll earn $15 for every verified Google review you capture. That gets added to your paycheck every two weeks. If you capture 10 reviews a month, that's an extra $150. Over a year, that's $1,800. And here's the bigger picture: more reviews = more warm leads = easier sales = higher commissions. So you're not just earning $15 per review—you're investing in your future income. We're also running a monthly contest: Whoever captures the most reviews each month wins a $100 gift card (or cash—your choice). We're going to track this on a leaderboard [point to whiteboard or show digital dashboard]. I'll update it every week. You'll be able to see exactly where you stand. And every week, we're going to celebrate wins publicly. When you capture a great review, I'm going to read it out loud at the team meeting and thank you by name. This isn't just about the money—it's about recognition and pride in the work you do. Does that sound fair?" [Let them respond. Get verbal agreement.]
4. Address Objections (10 minutes)

What to say:

"Okay, I know some of you might be thinking: 'What if the customer says no?' or 'What if I get a bad review?' or 'I'm too busy.' Let me address those right now. Objection #1: 'I don't want to be pushy.' You're not being pushy. You're giving the customer an opportunity to help the next family who's trying to decide where to go. That's not pushy—that's generous. And here's the reality: most customers are happy to leave a review. They're just waiting for someone to ask. Objection #2: 'What if we get a negative review?' The post-installation call is your insurance policy against bad reviews. When you call 24–48 hours after the job, you're catching small issues early and fixing them before they turn into big problems. And here's the thing: the best defense against a bad review is volume. If you have 150 five-star reviews and one two-star, nobody cares. But if you only have 20 reviews total, one bad one kills you. Objection #3: 'I'm too busy.' I get it. But the post-installation call takes 2 minutes. The text takes 30 seconds. That's less time than it takes to follow up on a cold lead three times. And every review you capture brings us more warm leads next month—which makes your job easier. So you're not too busy. You're investing 3 minutes now to save yourself hours later. Does anyone else have concerns or questions?" [Let them ask questions. Use the scripts from the Overcoming Barriers file to address any additional objections.]

Let's go out there and take care of our customers—and let's ask them to tell the world about it."

3. Troubleshoot and Offer Support (5 minutes)

What to say:

"Okay, now let's talk about what's hard. I know some of you are still getting comfortable with the script. That's normal. Who's struggling? What's hard about this?" [Let them share. Here's the fix: set a reminder on your phone for 9:00 AM every day. That's your cue to check yesterday's installs and make the calls. Do it first thing in the morning before anything else. Try the 'pay it forward' framing: 'When you leave a review, you're helping the next family who's trying to decide where to go—kind of like paying it forward.' That reframes it from 'help me' to 'help others,' and people respond to that." For "I don't know what to say": "That's why we gave you the scripts. Don't try to wing it—just follow the script. If you want, we can practice it together. Let's do a quick 2-minute role-play right now. I'll be the customer. You call me. Go." [Do a quick role-play if needed.]

  • Scenario: The customer says, "I don't really do reviews."

[Let them run through the objection-handling script.]

7. Wrap-Up and Key Takeaways (5 minutes)

What to say:

"Great job, everyone. Here are the key takeaways from today: • Make the post-installation call 24–48 hours after the job. This prevents 'Silent Death' and sets you up for a positive review. • Send the text immediately—don't wait. • If the customer hesitates, reframe the ask as 'paying it forward' and offer no pressure. • If you fixed a problem, ask for a review—it shows you stand behind your work, and customers often leave glowing reviews after you make something right. The more you practice, the more natural it will feel. And the more natural it feels, the more reviews you'll capture. Alright, let's go out there and capture some reviews this week. Who's with me?"

BONUS: Monthly "Review of the Month" Celebration

Goal: Create big, memorable moments that reinforce the behavior you want.
When to hold it: Last Friday of every month (10 minutes at end of team meeting)
What to do:
  1. Pick the single best review from the last 30 days.
  2. Choose one that's detailed, emotional, and mentions specific team members.
  3. Read it aloud to the entire team (sales, installers, office staff—everyone).
  4. Give the salesperson and installer a bonus ($50–$100 each) or gift card.
  5. Post it publicly:
    • Social media (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn)
    • Break room wall (print and frame it)
    • Email newsletter (if you send one)
  6. Say something like this: "This is the review of the month. This is what we're striving for every single time. [Salesperson Name] and [Installer Name]—you set the standard. Thank you."
Why this works:
  • Big, public celebrations create momentum and reinforce the behavior you want.
  • It shows the team that reviews aren't just numbers—they're stories about real people and real impact.
  • It creates friendly competition ("I want to win Review of the Month next time").

Final Thoughts: Consistency Beats Perfection

You don't need perfect meetings. You need consistent meetings.

If you follow this plan—especially the first 12 weeks—you'll build a review capture habit that becomes part of your team's DNA.

After Week 12, you're just maintaining. 5–10 minutes per week. Quick check-ins. Celebrate wins. Address bottlenecks. Set the goal.

That's it.

The reviews will compound. The leads will come. And your business will transform.
