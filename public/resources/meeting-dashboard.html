WEEKLY SALES MEETING DASHBOARD TEMPLATE

How to Track, Celebrate, and Drive Review Capture with a Simple Leaderboard

Introduction: Why You Need a Review Dashboard

If you don't track review capture, it won't happen consistently.

Your team needs to see:
  • Who's asking for reviews (and who's not)
  • Who's capturing the most reviews (friendly competition)
  • How the team is progressing toward the goal (momentum and accountability)

A Weekly Sales Meeting Dashboard is a simple visual tool (whiteboard, spreadsheet, or printed sheet) that tracks review capture by salesperson and makes it part of your weekly rhythm.

When you track it, celebrate it, and incentivize it, your team will do it.

Page 1

What Is the Weekly Sales Meeting Dashboard?

It's a one-page scoreboard that you update weekly and review at every sales meeting.

What it tracks:
  • Reviews captured per salesperson (this week)
  • Reviews captured per salesperson (this month)
  • Total reviews captured (all-time, per salesperson)
  • Team goal vs. actual results
  • Top performer of the week (optional: reward or recognition)
Where it lives:
  • Low-Lift Option: A whiteboard in your sales office or break room
  • Deep-Dive Option: A shared Google Sheet or Excel file that everyone can access

How to Track Reviews (Step-by-Step)

Here's how to keep your dashboard up to date.

Option A: Manual Tracking (Low-Lift)

Tools:

Whiteboard, printed sheet, or simple notebook

Action items:
  • Every Monday morning, update the leaderboard with last week's results
  • Ask each salesperson: "How many reviews did you capture last week?"
  • Update the whiteboard or printed sheet
  • At your Tuesday team meeting, review the results and celebrate wins

Time investment: 10 minutes per week

Option B: Digital Tracking (Deep-Dive)

Tools:

Google Sheets or Excel

Action items:
  • Create a shared Google Sheet with the dashboard layout (see template below)
  • Each salesperson enters their reviews into the sheet as they capture them (or you update it weekly based on what you see in your Google Business Profile)
  • The sheet automatically calculates totals, averages, and percentages
  • At your weekly team meeting, pull up the sheet on a screen and review the results

Time investment: 5 minutes per week (once the sheet is set up)

How to Gamify the Dashboard (Make It Fun)

People are motivated by recognition, competition, and rewards. Here's how to make your dashboard more engaging.

1. Weekly Prizes

What to do: The salesperson who captures the most reviews each week wins a small prize.

Prize ideas:
  • $50 cash or gift card
  • Free lunch
  • Extra PTO day
  • Reserved parking spot for the week
  • "Review Champion" trophy (that rotates week to week)

2. Monthly Competitions

What to do: At the end of each month, the salesperson with the most reviews wins a bigger prize.

Prize ideas:
  • $100‚Äì$200 cash or gift card
  • Dinner for two at a nice restaurant
  • Extra day off
  • Public recognition (e.g., "Salesperson of the Month" plaque)

3. Team Challenges

What to do: Set a team goal for the week or month. If the team hits the goal, everyone wins.

Prize ideas:
  • Catered lunch
  • Early close on Friday
  • Team outing (bowling, escape room, etc.)

Example:

"If we capture 40 reviews as a team this month, I'm taking everyone out to lunch at [restaurant]. Let's hit it!"

4. Visual Milestones

What to do: Mark major milestones on the leaderboard (e.g., 50 reviews, 100 reviews, 200 reviews) and celebrate them publicly.

Example:

"Team, we just hit 100 total reviews on our Google Business Profile! That's a huge milestone. Great work, everyone. I'm buying lunch on Friday to celebrate."

Pro Tips for Dashboard Success

1. Update It Weekly (Not Monthly)

If you only update the leaderboard once a month, it loses its power. Weekly updates create momentum and keep it top of mind.

2. Make It Visible

Put the leaderboard somewhere everyone can see it:

  • In the sales office (where salespeople gather)
  • In the break room (where installers and support staff can see it too)
  • On a shared screen in your showroom

3. Celebrate Publicly, Coach Privately

  • When someone does well, celebrate them in front of the team
  • When someone is struggling, pull them aside privately and ask: "What's hard about this? How can I help?"

4. Track Percentage of Jobs (Not Just Raw Numbers)

A salesperson who captures 5 reviews from 10 jobs (50% capture rate) is performing better than someone who captures 7 reviews from 20 jobs (35% capture rate).

Add this column to your dashboard:

Salesperson Jobs Completed Reviews Captured Capture Rate
Sarah 10 5 50%
Mike 20 7 35%

5. Don't Penalize‚ÄîIncentivize

Don't punish people for not capturing reviews. Instead, incentivize the behavior you want to see.

Bad: "Lisa, you didn't capture any reviews this week. That's unacceptable."

Good: "Lisa, I noticed you're at 0 this week. What's going on? Do you need help practicing the script? Let's role-play it together."
