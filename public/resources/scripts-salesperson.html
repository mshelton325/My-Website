REVIEW CAPTURE SCRIPTS – SALESPERSON EDITION

Word-for-Word Scripts for Every Customer Scenario

Introduction

This document contains the exact scripts you should use to capture Google reviews at every stage of the customer journey.

These scripts have been tested, refined, and proven to work. Don't reinvent the wheel—use these scripts, practice them until they feel natural, and adjust the wording slightly to match your voice.

Your job is simple: plant the seed, make the post-installation call, send the text, and follow up once if needed. If you do that consistently, you'll capture reviews from 40–60% of your customers—and you'll see the results in your paycheck.

SCRIPT 1: Planting the Seed (During the Initial Consultation)

When to use: During or at the end of the sales consultation, before the customer signs.

Purpose: Set the expectation that you'll ask for a review later—so it's not a surprise.

Script:

"By the way, one of the ways we've grown is through word of mouth and online reviews. If we do a great job for you—and I'm confident we will—I'll ask you to leave us a quick review on Google. Fair enough?"
Why this works:
  • It's conditional ("if we do a great job"), which builds confidence.
  • It removes the surprise factor—the customer already agreed.
  • It positions your company as successful and review-driven (social proof).

Time investment: 10 seconds.

SCRIPT 2: The Post-Installation Call (24–48 Hours After the Job)

When to use: 24–48 hours after installation is complete.

Purpose: Check for "Silent Death" issues (small problems the customer hasn't reported), fix them fast, and ask for the review while the positive experience is fresh.

Script (If Everything Went Well):

"Hi [Customer Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. I'm just calling to check in and make sure everything went smoothly with your installation. How's the floor looking? Any questions or concerns I can help with?"

[Customer says everything's great]

"That's great to hear! I'm so glad we could take care of you. Now, I know you're probably busy, but if you have a minute this week, I'd REALLY appreciate it if you could leave us a quick review on Google. It helps us out a ton and helps other people feel confident choosing us. I'm going to text you a link right now so it's easy—does that work?"

[Customer agrees]

"Perfect! You'll see the text in just a second. Thanks so much—we really appreciate you!"

Script (If There's a Small Issue):

"Hi [Customer Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. I'm just calling to check in and make sure everything went smoothly with your installation. How's the floor looking? Any questions or concerns I can help with?"

[Customer mentions a small issue—e.g., a transition strip is crooked, a spot needs touch-up, etc.]

"Thanks so much for letting me know. I really appreciate you telling me—that's exactly the kind of thing I want to catch early. I'm going to get that taken care of right away. Let me call you back once it's fixed, and then I'll send you that review link. Sound good?"

[Fix the issue, then call back]

"Hi [Customer Name], this is [Your Name] again. Just wanted to let you know we took care of that [issue]. Everything should be perfect now. If you're happy with how we handled it, I'd love it if you could leave us a quick review on Google. I'm going to text you the link right now. Does that work?"

Why this works:
  • You're demonstrating that you care about quality and follow-through (which makes them want to leave a good review).
  • You're catching issues early, before they escalate into bad reviews.
  • You're asking for the review while the positive experience is still fresh.

Time investment: 2 minutes per call.

SCRIPT 3: The Text Message (Sent Immediately After the Call)

When to use: Immediately after the post-installation call, while you're still on the phone or right after you hang up.

Purpose: Get the review link into the customer's hands so there's no friction.

Text Template (Standard Version):

"Hi [Customer Name], thanks again for choosing us! Here's the link to leave your review: [Google Review Link]. It'll only take a minute, and it really helps us out. We appreciate you!"

Text Template (Pay It Forward Version):

"Hi [Customer Name], thanks again for trusting us with your project! If you have a minute, we'd love it if you could leave us a quick Google review. When you do, you're helping the next family who's trying to decide where to go—kind of like paying it forward. Here's the link: [Google Review Link]. We really appreciate you!"
Why this works:
  • The link is in their hand. There's no friction.
  • They can do it right now if they want, or later when they have time.
  • The "pay it forward" framing makes it feel altruistic, not transactional.

Time investment: 30 seconds.

SCRIPT 4: The Follow-Up Text (3–5 Days Later)

When to use: If the customer hasn't left a review within 3–5 days of your initial text.

Purpose: Give them a gentle, helpful reminder.

Follow-Up Text:

"Hi [Customer Name], just checking in—did the link I sent work okay? I know things get busy, but if you get a chance, we'd love that review! Here's the link again: [Google Review Link]. Let me know if you have any trouble with it. Thanks!"
Why this works:
  • You're not saying, "Hey, you forgot." You're saying, "Did the link work?"
  • It's a helpful, low-pressure nudge that often gets results.
  • The actionable question ("did the link work?") prompts a response and reduces customer guilt.

Time investment: 30 seconds.

SCRIPT 5: Handling Customer Objections

Sometimes customers will hesitate or push back. Here's how to handle the most common objections.

Objection #1: "I don't really do reviews."

Response:
"I totally understand—not everyone does. But I'll tell you, it really does help us out. And it also helps the next family who's trying to decide where to go—kind of like paying it forward. If you have a minute, I'd really appreciate it. No pressure, though!"

Objection #2: "I'll do it later."

Response:
"That's great, thank you! I'm going to text you the link right now so you have it whenever you're ready. It only takes about 60 seconds. Thanks so much!"

[Send the text immediately. Then follow up 3–5 days later if they don't leave the review.]

Objection #3: "I'm not tech-savvy / I don't know how."

Response:
"No problem at all! It's really easy—I'll text you a link, and all you have to do is click it, rate us with stars, and write a sentence or two about your experience. If you have any trouble, just call me back and I'll walk you through it. Sound good?"

Objection #4: "What if something goes wrong later?"

Response:
"That's exactly why I'm calling you now—to make sure everything's perfect before we ask for a review. If anything comes up later, you can always call us and we'll take care of it. That's part of our commitment to you. But if everything looks good right now, I'd love it if you could leave us a review. Does that sound fair?"

Objection #5: "I already recommended you to my neighbor."

Response:
"That's awesome—thank you so much! And honestly, an online review is kind of like recommending us to a hundred neighbors at once. It helps other people find us the same way you did. If you have a minute, I'd really appreciate it!"

SCRIPT 6: Asking in Person (If the Customer Comes Back to Your Showroom)

When to use: If the customer stops by your showroom after the job is done (to pick up leftover materials, ask a question, etc.).

Purpose: Ask for the review face-to-face.

Script:

"Hey [Customer Name]! Great to see you. How's the floor looking? Everything holding up well?"

[Customer says yes]

"That's great! Hey, real quick—if you have a minute, I'd love it if you could leave us a Google review. It helps us out a ton. I can text you the link right now, or if you have your phone handy, I can pull it up for you. Which works better?"

Why this works:
  • You're asking in person, which is harder to say no to.
  • You're offering to make it easy (text the link or pull it up right there).

SCRIPT 7: Asking After You've Fixed a Problem

When to use: After you've resolved a small issue the customer reported.

Purpose: Turn a potential negative into a positive by showing you care and follow through.

Script:

"Hi [Customer Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. Just wanted to let you know we took care of that [issue you mentioned]. Everything should be perfect now. Are you happy with how we handled it?"

[Customer says yes]

"That's great to hear. I'm really glad we could make it right. Now, if you're comfortable with it, I'd love it if you could leave us a review on Google. It helps other people know that if something does come up, we'll take care of it fast. I'm going to text you the link right now. Does that work?"

Why this works:
  • Customers often leave glowing reviews after you fix a problem—because it shows you care.
  • You're framing the review as proof that you stand behind your work.

Pro Tips for Script Success

  • Practice them out loud. Don't just read the scripts—role-play them until they feel natural.
  • Adjust the wording to match your voice. These scripts are proven, but you can tweak them slightly to sound like you.
  • Don't skip the post-installation call. This is the most important step. It prevents "Silent Death" and sets you up for a positive review.
  • Send the text immediately. Don't wait. Don't say "I'll send it later." Send it while you're on the phone.
  • Follow up once. If they don't leave a review within 3–5 days, send one friendly follow-up. After that, let it go.
  • Celebrate every win. When someone leaves a review, thank them—and ask your manager to recognize it in team meetings. Positive reinforcement builds momentum.
