The Simple System: One Review, One Post, Once a Week

Here's the entire system in one sentence: Pick one great review each week, screenshot it, write a short caption, and post it on Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn.

STEP 1: PICK YOUR BEST REVIEW FROM THE WEEK

Every Monday (or whatever day you update your Review Leaderboard), look at the reviews you captured in the last 7 days.

Pick one that:
  • Is detailed and specific (not just "Great service!")
  • Mentions something concrete (installers, cleanliness, communication, quality, etc.)
  • Has emotional impact ("We're so happy!" or "Best experience ever!")
  • Optional: Mentions a team member by name (great for recognition)
Time investment: 2 minutes.

STEP 2: SCREENSHOT THE REVIEW

Option A: Screenshot Directly from Google (Low-Lift)

  1. Go to your Google Business Profile.
  2. Find the review.
  3. Take a screenshot on your phone or computer.
  4. Crop the screenshot so it only shows:
    • The customer's name (first name + last initial)
    • The star rating
    • The review text
    • The date (optional)
Time investment: 1 minute.

Option B: Create a Branded Graphic (Deep-Dive)

If you want your posts to look more polished, use a tool like Canva to create a branded review graphic.

Action items:
  1. Go to Canva.com (free version works fine).
  2. Create a new design (square format: 1080 x 1080 pixels for Instagram, or 1200 x 630 for Facebook).
  3. Choose a simple template or create your own:
    • Background: Your brand colors or a photo of a completed job
    • Text: Paste the review text
    • Logo: Add your company logo in the corner
    • Star rating: Add 5 gold stars at the top
  4. Download the image and save it.
Time investment: 5 minutes (first time). Once you create a template, you can reuse it every week in 2 minutes.

Pro Tip: Create 2‚Äì3 Canva templates and rotate through them so your posts don't all look identical.

Advanced Strategies (Optional)

Once you've built the habit of posting one review per week, you can layer in these advanced tactics.

1. Tag Your Team Members (If They're on Social Media)

If the review mentions a salesperson or installer by name, and they have a Facebook or Instagram account, tag them in the post.

Why this works:
  • It gives them public recognition.
  • It shows prospective customers that real people work at your company.
  • It boosts engagement (their friends and family will see the post and like/comment).

2. Post on Instagram Stories (In Addition to Your Feed)

Instagram Stories are quick, casual, and high-engagement.

How to do it:
  1. Post the review graphic to your Instagram feed (as usual).
  2. Then, share it to your Instagram Story with a sticker or handwritten note:
    • "We love our customers! üíô"
    • "This made our day!"
  3. Add a "Swipe Up" link (if you have 10K+ followers) or just tell people to check your profile for more info.
Time investment: 1 minute.

3. Create a "Review Highlight" Reel on Instagram

Instagram lets you save Stories to "Highlights" that stay on your profile permanently.

How to do it:
  1. Create a Highlight called "Reviews" or "Happy Customers."
  2. Every time you post a review to your Story, add it to the Highlight.
  3. Over time, you'll build a library of reviews that new followers can scroll through.

Why this works: It's social proof that lives on your profile 24/7.

4. Post a "Review of the Month" Roundup

At the end of each month, create a single post that highlights 3‚Äì5 of your best reviews from that month.

How to do it:
  1. Use Canva to create a multi-slide carousel post (Instagram) or a single graphic (Facebook/LinkedIn).
  2. Include short snippets from 3‚Äì5 reviews.
  3. Caption: "Here are some of our favorite reviews from [Month]. Thanks to everyone who took the time to share their experience! ‚ù§Ô∏è"
Time investment: 10 minutes per month.

5. Repurpose Reviews for Email Newsletters

If you send a monthly email newsletter to past customers or leads, include a "Customer Spotlight" section with 1‚Äì2 reviews.

Why this works: It reminds past customers that you're still doing great work (which leads to referrals) and builds trust with leads who haven't bought yet.

6. Share Before-and-After Photos with Reviews

If you have a great before-and-after photo of a completed job, pair it with a review from that customer.

How to do it:
  1. Create a side-by-side before-and-after graphic in Canva.
  2. Add the customer's review as text below or on top of the image.
  3. Post it with a caption like: "From outdated to amazing! Here's what [Customer Name] had to say..."

Why this works: Visual transformation + social proof = high engagement.

What NOT to Do

‚ùå Don't Post Every Single Review

If you post reviews every day, it feels spammy and people will tune out. Once a week is perfect.

‚ùå Don't Copy-Paste the Exact Same Caption Every Week

Vary your captions so they don't feel robotic. Use the 5 templates above and rotate through them.

‚ùå Don't Ignore Comments

If someone comments on your post ("Congrats!" or "Love this!"), take 10 seconds to reply: "Thanks so much!" or "We appreciate you!"

Why this matters: Engagement signals to Facebook and Instagram that your content is valuable, so they'll show it to more people.

‚ùå Don't Overthink It

You don't need professional photography, fancy graphics, or viral captions. Simple, genuine posts work best.

Tracking: How to Measure Impact

You don't need to obsess over metrics, but here are a few simple ways to track whether your social media posts are working:

1. Engagement (Likes, Comments, Shares)

Are people engaging with your review posts?

Goal: At least 10‚Äì20 likes and 2‚Äì5 comments per post.
If your engagement is low:
  • Post at better times (e.g., weekday mornings or evenings when people are scrolling)
  • Tag team members in the post
  • Ask a question in the caption (e.g., "What's your favorite thing about new floors?")

2. New Followers

Are you gaining followers on Facebook or Instagram?

Goal: Slow, steady growth (5‚Äì10 new followers per month is fine for a local business).

3. Lead Source

When a new customer calls or fills out a form, ask: "How did you hear about us?"

If they say "I saw you on Facebook" or "I follow you on Instagram," make a note of it.

Why this matters: It tells you that your social media presence is contributing to lead generation.
