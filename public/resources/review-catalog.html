STEP 3: TAG YOUR REVIEWS BY THEME

This is the most important step. You're going to read through each review and assign tags based on what the customer talks about.

How to Tag Reviews:

Read the review and ask yourself:

  • What is the customer praising?
  • What concern or objection does this review address?
  • What theme(s) does this review fit into?

Then add those tags to the "Tags / Themes" column.

Example:

Review:
"The installers were so professional and cleaned up everything. I was shocked at how spotless they left the room! And when we had a small issue with one of the transition strips, they came back the next day and fixed it. Great service."

Tags:

Installers Cleanliness Professionalism Problem Resolution Accountability

Recommended Tag Categories:

Here are the most common themes to use:

  • Quality / Craftsmanship â€“ Reviews that praise the quality of the work, attention to detail, or long-term durability.
  • Price / Value / ROI â€“ Reviews that address price objections or talk about why the customer chose you despite being more expensive.
  • Salesperson / Customer Service â€“ Reviews that mention the salesperson by name or praise the sales experience.
  • Installers / Installation Quality â€“ Reviews that praise the installers' skill, professionalism, or work ethic.
  • Cleanliness / Professionalism â€“ Reviews that talk about how clean the installers left the job site.
  • Communication / Follow-Through â€“ Reviews that praise your responsiveness, follow-up, or project management.
  • Problem Resolution / Accountability â€“ Reviews where you fixed an issue and the customer appreciated how you handled it.
  • Product Selection / Showroom Experience â€“ Reviews that talk about your showroom, product variety, or design consultation.
  • Speed / Timeliness â€“ Reviews that praise fast turnaround, on-time installation, or quick responses.
  • Overall Experience â€“ General "we loved everything" reviews that don't fit into a specific category.

STEP 5: KEEP YOUR REVIEW CATALOG UPDATED

Your Review Catalog is a living document. As you earn new reviews, add them to the catalog and tag them.

Recommended rhythm:
  • Weekly: Export any new reviews and add them to the catalog.
  • Monthly: Review the catalog and identify which themes you need more reviews for. If you only have 2 reviews about "Cleanliness," make it a priority to ask customers to mention that in their reviews.

Pro Tips for Maximum Impact

1. Train Your Team to Use It

Your Review Catalog is only valuable if your team actually uses it.

Action items:

  • Show your salespeople where the catalog lives (Google Drive, CRM, etc.).
  • Walk them through 2â€“3 examples of how to use it.
  • Role-play a scenario where they need to handle an objection and send curated reviews.
2. Make It Easy to Access

Don't bury the Review Catalog in a folder no one can find.

Recommendations:

  • Save it in a shared Google Sheet so everyone has access.
  • Bookmark the link or pin it to your CRM.
  • Print a one-page "Quick Reference" guide with your top 10 reviews (organized by theme) and give it to every salesperson.
3. Prioritize Your Best Reviews

Not all reviews are created equal. Some are short and vague ("Great service!"). Others are detailed and specific ("The installers were on time, professional, and cleaned up everything. When we had a small issue, they came back the next day and fixed it.").

Focus on the detailed, specific reviews. Those are the ones that address real concerns and build credibility.

4. Don't Overwhelm Customers

When you're sending reviews to a customer, don't send 20. Send 2â€“3 that directly address their concern.

Why this works: It's focused, relevant, and easy to digest. Sending 20 reviews feels like spam.

5. Update Tags as Your Business Evolves

As your business grows, you may need to add new tags.

Examples:

  • If you start doing commercial projects, add a "Commercial" tag.
  • If you hire a new salesperson and customers start mentioning them by name, add a tag for that salesperson.
