WEEK 1: SETUP & KICKOFF

Time Required: 3‚Äì5 hours total for manager

Monday: Audit Your Current Google Business Profile (30 minutes)

Action items:
  • Go to Google and search for your business name + city
  • Review your Google Business Profile: How many reviews do you have? What's your average star rating? When was your last review posted?

Tuesday: Generate Your Google Review Link (15 minutes)

Action items:
  • Follow the instructions in the Review Request Text Templates file to generate your unique Google review link

Wednesday: Customize Scripts and Templates (1 hour)

Action items:

Thursday: Set Up Tracking (1 hour)

Action items:

WEEK 6 (OPTIONAL): LAUNCH THE INSTALLER CARD SYSTEM

Time Required: 1 hour (one-time setup)

What it is:

Physical business cards that your installers hand to customers at the end of the job, asking to be mentioned by name in the review.

Why it works:

  • It personalizes the review request (customers feel connected to the installer, not just the company)
  • It gives installers ownership and pride in their work
  • It increases the likelihood that customers leave detailed, specific reviews

How to set it up:

  • Design a simple business card with installer's name, photo (optional), "Please mention me by name in your review!"
  • Print 50‚Äì100 cards per installer (cheap via VistaPrint, Canva, or local print shop)
  • Train installers to hand the card to the customer at the end of the job
  • Optional: Pay installers $25 for every review that mentions them by name

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Pitfall #1: Giving Up After a Slow Start

The first month is always the hardest. Your team is learning. Customers are hesitant. Momentum is low. Stick with it. By month two or three, it becomes second nature.

Pitfall #2: Not Tracking Consistently

If you stop updating the leaderboard or celebrating wins, the team assumes it doesn't matter‚Äîand they stop doing it.

Pitfall #3: Not Making the Post-Installation Call

This is the most important step. If you skip it, your response rate will be much lower‚Äîand you'll miss "Silent Death" issues.

Pitfall #4: Asking for Reviews on Multiple Platforms

Focus on Google first. Once you have 100+ Google reviews, then you can branch out to Facebook, Yelp, etc.

Pitfall #5: Not Paying Out Incentives

If you announce a $15-per-review bonus and then don't pay it, your team will lose trust and stop asking.
